About SILENCE

A brand built by musicians, for musicians — made to help artists pass their hard-won wisdom on to the people who will make the next sound.

Home/About

Everyone wants the noise. We came for the thing underneath it — otherwise, I hear.

SILENCE makes the gear that sits between an artist and the sound: studio tools, considered apparel and accessories for the people who actually make music. But the kit is only half of it. Around the product we built a place to listen — a journal where engineers, writers and players hand down what took them years to learn, so the next person spends less time guessing and more time creating. Wisdom over hype. That is the whole brief.

How it started

SILENCE began in 2026, in a half-soundproofed room above a record shop, founded by a producer, a session player and a writer who had spent a decade swapping notes the industry never bothered to write down. They had watched too much knowledge vanish — the trick that fixed a mix at 3am, the lesson learned the expensive way on a first tour. The gear they could buy anywhere. The wisdom, nobody was selling.

So they built a brand that did both. The first run was a single condenser mic and a black tee with one line printed inside the collar: otherwise, I hear. It sold out before the launch email finished sending. The kit funds the journal; the journal feeds the kit. Two seasons in, that loop is still the whole company.

Our values

Listen first

Before we design anything, we ask the people who will use it. Every mic, every garment, every article starts with an hour of quiet attention to someone who knows more than we do. The brand is named for it because it is the point.

Craft over hype

We would rather ship one tool that lasts a career than ten that trend for a week. No marketing-led releases, no specs added to win an argument — only the details a working musician would actually thank us for.

Share the wisdom

Knowledge kept is knowledge wasted. We pay artists to write down what they know and we give it away freely, because the industry gets better when its hard lessons stop being secrets.

Back the artist

The maker comes before the brand, always. Fair terms for contributors, honest pricing on gear, and a platform that exists to amplify other people's voices — not drown them out with our own.
