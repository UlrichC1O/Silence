Sound & Responsibility

Responsibility

Making sound carries weight — for the artists we serve, the materials we choose, and the next generation learning to play. This is how SILENCE holds itself to account, in numbers we publish and keep.

Home/Responsibility

A brand for musicians owes a debt to the people who make the music — otherwise, I hear.

We sell gear to the people who shape sound, and we believe that comes with obligations: pay artists fairly, build with materials that do not cost the earth, and put real money back into music education. Every figure below is reviewed each quarter and reported in full — the shortfalls listed beside the wins, because wisdom does not flatter itself.

Our pillars

Four disciplines hold the whole programme together. Each has its own team, its own budget, and its own line on the audit.

Materials

From the cotton in a tee to the foam in a flight case, we start every product with a lower-impact brief: recycled polyester, organic cotton, FSC-certified packaging and finishes free of single-use plastic. Sound should not cost the planet its silence.

People

Every factory partner is audited against a published code on fair wages, safe hours and the right to organise. We visit the people who cut and stitch our gear, and we name the suppliers we work with — transparency is the only honest setting.

Artists

Our fair royalties pledge guarantees musicians clear, prompt and generous terms on every collaboration and sampled voice. No buried clauses, no rights grabs — the people who make the sound keep their share of it.

Community

Five percent of annual profit goes to music education — instruments, tuition and studio time for young players who would never otherwise reach a microphone. The next generation of wisdom has to be funded, not just hoped for.

The road ahead

A timeline we are accountable to, in public — reported each year alongside the figures that prove it.

2026

The baseline, in the open

First independently audited footprint published across our operations and supply chain. Carbon-neutral shipping live on 100% of orders, and the fair artist royalties pledge signed into every contract. The clock officially starts.

2027

Suppliers named and mapped

Full supplier list published, with each factory rated against our labour code. The first SILENCE Sound Schools open in three cities, funded entirely from the music-education pledge.

2028

80% better materials

At least four in five products built from recycled or organic materials, without trading away the quality the studio demands. Plastic-free packaging across the entire range.

2029

Take-back everywhere

A repair-and-recycle scheme for apparel and accessories goes live in every market we sell in, so no SILENCE product reaches the end of its life without a second chance.

2030

Ten percent, ten thousand players

The music-education pledge doubles to ten percent of profit, reaching ten thousand young musicians a year. Absolute emissions halved against our 2026 baseline. The work that matters most, made permanent.

Sound Schools

The SILENCE Sound Schools

Our flagship programme puts instruments, tuition and free studio time into the hands of young players in communities where music funding has gone quiet. Run with local teachers and working artists, each school pairs a learner with a mentor and the gear to find their own voice. Funded entirely from our profit pledge, it has reached more than two thousand students across three cities — and every pair of hands it helps is one more story the industry gets to hear.
